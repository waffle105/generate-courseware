# Visual Courseware Patterns

Use these patterns to match the user's preferred teaching decks: full-slide PNGs, low text density, clear logic, and design that serves the lesson.

## Universal Patterns

- Canvas: 1920x1080, 16:9, full-bleed image per slide.
- Structure: one core idea per page, expressed through a diagram or scene.
- Text: short Chinese titles and labels only; avoid paragraph explanations on the slide.
- Teaching value: the instructor notes carry explanation depth; the image carries memory and logic.
- Layout: strong hierarchy, generous spacing, readable labels, visible arrows or relationships.
- Repetition: keep recurring modules, colors, icon style, and footer markers consistent inside one deck.

## Tech HUD Style

Use for AI tools, skills, workflows, data, automation, and technical operation courses.

- Palette: dark navy or black base, electric blue/cyan highlights, occasional white emphasis.
- Motifs: dashboards, HUD panels, circular engines, process pipes, radar charts, checklists, data cards.
- Best for: method frameworks, step-by-step operations, comparisons, quality criteria, tool selection.
- Avoid: excessive decorative charts that do not teach anything.

## Default Blue Tech Consulting Style

Use this as the default when the user does not specify a style. It is based on the provided blue technology enterprise-management PPT chart reference.

- Palette: deep blue/black background, bright electric-blue light paths, cyan glows, white high-contrast titles.
- Layout: large left title area, structured right-side or center content modules, subtle world-map/grid/data texture.
- Components: translucent glass panels, rounded rectangles, thin neon strokes, icon circles, dashboard cards, progress lines, matrix blocks.
- Typography feel: large high-contrast Chinese title, smaller English subtitle when useful, consistent body-label scale across all content pages.
- Page system: cover, contents, chapter divider, and content pages should share the same background language, title position, glow intensity, panel corner radius, and footer/page-number style.
- Use for: business, management, strategy, AI, digitalization, marketing consulting, and professional training when no stronger industry-specific style is requested.
- Avoid: copying any source logo, using stock watermark text, or changing title size/position from page to page.

## Rural Content Production Style

Use for agriculture, local culture, rural commerce, cultural tourism, creator economy, and warm public-facing examples.

- Palette: warm paper, green, gold, rice, bamboo, natural daylight.
- Motifs: fields, local products, phones, editing screens, packaging, village scenes, hand-drawn arrows.
- Best for: content production pipelines, case maps, product promotion, community storytelling.
- Avoid: overly nostalgic decoration that hides the process or makes the slide look like a poster only.

## Film Production Style

Use for AI video, short films, storyboards, creative planning, prompting, and editing workflows.

- Palette: cinematic black, gold, blue accents, warm studio light.
- Motifs: clapboards, storyboard sheets, lens panels, film strips, production checklists, shot types.
- Best for: story-first methods, camera language, asset preparation, prompt structure, editing checks.
- Avoid: overly realistic small UI panels with illegible text.

## Diagram Choices

- Process or workflow: horizontal pipeline, circular loop, subway map, production line.
- Selection or filtering: funnel, scorecard, decision tree, comparison matrix.
- Concept relationship: hub-and-spoke, layered stack, orbit diagram.
- Practice page: worksheet-like visual with blanks, numbered slots, and a clear output area.
- Summary: checklist, map of the whole course, or compact framework poster.

## Consistency Rules

- Lock a style guide after the user chooses one of the two sample directions.
- Keep title placement, margins, content grid, panel size rhythm, icon line weight, glow intensity, background texture, and page-number treatment consistent.
- Let page roles vary, but keep their family resemblance: chapter dividers, framework pages, case pages, practice pages, and summary pages should each have a stable grammar.
- If a later slide needs a new diagram form, adapt it to the selected style rather than inventing a new visual system.

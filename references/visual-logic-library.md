# Visual Logic Library

Use this library to turn abstract course content into clear visual structures. Choose the structure that best matches the teaching intent; do not force every slide into the same chart type.

## Strategy Analysis

Use for enterprise strategy, industry analysis, and business planning.

- SWOT analysis: strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, threats.
- PEST analysis: political, economic, social, technological environment.
- Porter's five forces: industry competition and market pressure.
- Strategy map: strategic path from goal to execution.
- Competitive matrix: brand or company market position.
- Strategy pyramid: vision, goals, strategy, actions.
- Growth flywheel: reinforcing growth logic.
- Second curve model: new business growth path.
- Strategy decomposition: strategy to execution layers.
- Portfolio matrix: star, cash cow, question, and dog businesses.

## Business Model

Use for financing decks, company introductions, and project explanations.

- Business model canvas: customers, value, channels, revenue, and resources.
- Value chain: how the organization creates value.
- Ecosystem map: platform, customers, partners, and resources.
- Revenue model: revenue sources and monetization paths.
- Cost structure: cost components and optimization direction.
- Platform model: B-side, C-side, supply side, and service side connections.
- Closed-loop business model: traffic, conversion, repurchase, referral.
- Moat model: brand, technology, channel, data, and cost advantages.
- Supply-demand matching model: platform business logic.
- Industry chain map: upstream, midstream, downstream relationships.

## Marketing Strategy

Use for marketing consulting, branding, and growth plans.

- STP model: segmentation, targeting, positioning.
- 4P model: product, price, place, promotion.
- 4C model: customer, cost, convenience, communication.
- Brand positioning map: differentiation position.
- Brand pyramid: functional, emotional, and symbolic value.
- Brand asset model: awareness, recognition, reputation, loyalty.
- Marketing funnel: exposure, interest, conversion, purchase.
- AARRR model: acquisition, activation, retention, revenue, referral.
- User journey map: awareness to purchase.
- Content marketing matrix: content type matched to communication goal.

## User And Market Insight

Use for market research, consumer analysis, and user operations.

- Persona card: demographics, needs, pain points, behaviors.
- User segmentation model: new, active, dormant, high-value users.
- Consumer decision path: need to purchase decision.
- Customer lifecycle: acquisition, growth, maturity, churn, recall.
- Needs pyramid: basic to advanced needs.
- Pain point analysis: scenario, problem, cause, opportunity.
- Behavior path: visit, click, consult, order.
- Market size funnel: TAM, SAM, SOM.
- Target customer screening matrix: high-value customer identification.
- Customer value matrix: value and potential.

## Growth And Conversion

Use for growth consulting, private traffic, ecommerce, and internet products.

- Growth funnel: traffic to transaction.
- Private-domain loop: acquisition, retention, conversion, repurchase.
- Referral growth model: seed users, invitation, diffusion, return.
- Conversion path: touchpoints that lead to purchase.
- Traffic source structure: public, private, search, recommendation.
- Repurchase model: first purchase, repeat purchase, membership, loyalty.
- Growth lever map: product, channel, content, data, operations.
- Retention curve: sustained usage over time.
- Member growth system: normal user to super user.
- Data-driven growth model: collect, analyze, strategize, optimize.

## Organization Management

Use for management consulting, organization change, and internal reporting.

- Organization chart: department and role relationships.
- Matrix organization: function line and project line collaboration.
- Capability model: strategy, talent, process, culture.
- Talent ladder: junior, intermediate, senior, expert.
- Responsibility matrix: role boundaries.
- RACI matrix: responsible, accountable, consulted, informed.
- Performance loop: goal, execution, feedback, improvement.
- Change roadmap: current state, transformation, implementation, optimization.
- Culture model: mission, vision, values, behavior.
- Leadership model: decision, execution, influence, innovation.

## Process Management

Use for operations, project management, and digital transformation.

- Business process map: start-to-end workflow.
- Service blueprint: front stage, back stage, support systems.
- End-to-end process: customer need to delivered result.
- SOP flowchart: standard operating steps.
- Swimlane diagram: cross-department collaboration.
- Problem diagnosis flow: discover, locate cause, propose solution.
- Decision tree: conditional paths.
- Closed-loop management: plan, do, check, optimize.
- Digital process: data, systems, people, business coordination.
- Operations middle platform: front office, middle office, back office.

## Project Management

Use for consulting plans, implementation plans, and proposals.

- Gantt chart: project timeline.
- Milestone map: key nodes.
- Roadmap: phased plan.
- Implementation framework: goal, path, resources, outcomes.
- Risk matrix: probability and impact.
- Priority matrix: importance and urgency.
- Resource allocation map: people, budget, time, tools.
- Governance structure: decision, management, execution layers.
- Stage deliverables map: outputs by phase.

## Business Performance Analysis

Use for annual reports, business reviews, and commercial analysis.

- Revenue structure: revenue share by business.
- Profit analysis: revenue, cost, gross profit, net profit.
- Cost breakdown: fixed, variable, labor costs.
- Business dashboard: core metrics overview.
- KPI tree: total goal to department metrics.
- DuPont analysis: financial performance decomposition.
- Business diagnosis: problem, cause, impact, recommendation.
- Growth waterfall: contribution of growth factors.
- Budget execution: budget, actual, variance.
- Business loop: goal, resource, execution, result, feedback.

Use for AI courses, digital transformation, AI operations, and AI marketing.

- AI-enabled business model: how AI embeds into workflows.
- Data middle-platform architecture: collection, governance, application.
